Biography

Laura Young is one of the most expressive artists in the world of classical guitar today. Born in Toronto of Russian and Irish heritage, Laura began her study of music at the age of seven. With a rare passion and intensity she applied herself to technical mastery of the instrument, building on the exceptional musicality evident from the moment she began to play. Laura has been recognized as a gifted artist; winning prestigious prizes at international competitions such as the “Andrés Segovia” in Palma de Mallorca, Spain and the “Jacinto e Inocencio Guererro” in Madrid, Spain, among many others. As a soloist Laura has given widely acclaimed performances, offering profound and often daring programs in concert halls around the world, from the Alte Oper in Frankfurt, Germany to the National Arts Centre in Ottawa, Canada to the Auditorio Nacional in Madrid, Spain. She’s also a regularly invited performer and teacher in International Guitar Festivals: Toronto, Canada; Ezstergom, Hungary; Gdansk, Poland; Cologne, Germany; and Volos, Greece – to name but a few. In addition to her solo career, Laura was a founding member of the very successful Trio de Cologne with Zoran Dukić and Pablo Marquez who performed throughout the 1990’s in Europe and the Americas. Numerous composers have dedicated works to the trio including: Sergio Assad, Carlo Domeniconi, Atanas Ourkouzounov and Dušan Bogdanović among others. Laura is known and appreciated by critics and the listening public alike for the power and beauty of her sound, depth of colour and exquisite phrasing in her playing. She has recorded a number of recordings with a richly varied repertoire, most recently works by Max Reger for solo violin and solo cello which received the prestigious Pasticcio Critics Award in Austria 2016. Her duo Lagrimosa Beltá with mezzo soprano Frances Pappas, is considered one of the best ensemble of its kind offering ancient songs in contemporary settings. From 1997 - 2001, Laura was the founder and Artistic Director of the Amsterdam International Guitar Festival. Laura Young was awarded full professorship at the Escuela Superior de Musica en Catalunya (ESMUC) in Barcelona, Spain in 2003. In October 2014, she was appointed professor of guitar at the Mozarteum in Salzburg, Austria.
